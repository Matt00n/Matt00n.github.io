The goal of my research is to bring artificial intelligence closer to deployment in everyday life by discovering new and exciting applications of deep reinforcement learning. Within this field, I am particularly interested in human-in-the-loop reinforcement learning. The complexity of humans dynamically interacting with such intelligent systems gives rise to new sets of questions but also the potential for artificial intelligence that is save and tailored to our preferences.

[Working Title] Novel application of reinforcement learning from human preferences

Ongoing project

In this project, we propose a novel application at the intersection of natural language processing and reinforcement learning from human preferences. For this purpose, we leverage pretrained large language models and infuse human insights through reinforcement learning into the model.

[Working Title] Robustly applying deep reinforcement learning to inventory management

Ongoing project

In this project, we are aiming to bring reinforcement learning closer to a real-world application in inventory management. We specifically train the model to be robust against unforeseeable real-world circumstances and interactions with potentially non-rational human collaborators. Among others, this is achieved via a human-in-the-loop reinforcement learning approach.
[Kaggle Competition] Happywhale - Whale and Dolphin Identification

Bronze medal [code]

In this computer vision competition, the goal was to identify marine mammals among a total of over 15,000 individuums. Our proposed solution uses an EfficientNet backbone with an ArcFace head. For inference, we compute nearest neighbors in the training set using on the embeddings from our model.

Kaggle Competition: Happywhale - Whale and Dolphin Identification

Bronze medal [code]

In this project during my internship at REWE, we built a transformer-based classification model to allocate products to the correct category among over 1,000 options based on their text identifiers (mixtures of product name abbreviations, numeric information and special characters). By finetuning BERT on the preprocessed identifiers, we achieved human-expert level accuracy and largely minimized the manual effort for domain experts by automatically allocating over 100,000 products to appropriate categories.
